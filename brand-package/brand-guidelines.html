The Coin

The mark is a coin: the numeral 8 and the letter E — equal weight, equal size — joined by a single canted stroke, set in Playfair Display inside a double ring. It is finished antiqued, not crisp: the rings carry a faint hand-printed irregularity and the surface a soft paper grain, so it reads as something pressed and aged rather than machine-perfect. That warmth is the point. Keep it pure — never place text inside the ring.

Primary
Navy on cream. Default everywhere.

Two finishes, one mark. The antiqued cut is for feature sizes — hero, print, letterhead, the cream primary. At 16–32 px (favicon, app icon) the grain disappears and the edge texture muddies, so those use the clean cut below. Same coin, finished for its size.

The clean cut — favicon & small UI, no texture, inner ring intact down to 24 px and dropped below 16 px.

Clear space & minimum size. Clear space equals the ring’s inner radius on all sides. Minimum full-coin size 28 px.
02

Lockups

The coin is the icon; the lockup is the full signature. The masthead is Playfair to match the coin, with MEDIA as a Hanken-tracked descriptor beneath — never inside the ring. The antiqued finish carries across the whole lockup so coin and wordmark read as one printed piece.

Stacked · Primary
Stacked · Reversed
Do
Keep coin and masthead in one serif (Playfair) and one gold. Preserve the antiqued finish at feature size. Give the lockup room.
Don’t
Recolor off-palette, sharpen the coin to hard vector for feature use, stretch or rotate, or set MEDIA inside the ring.
03

Typography

Two faces, one voice. Playfair Display — a high-contrast display serif — carries the coin, mastheads, and headlines; its drama is the brand’s personality. Hanken Grotesk handles body, UI, and labels: warm enough to feel human, neutral enough to disappear into the reading. We deliberately avoid the default web sans so the brand doesn’t blend into every other agency.

The signature
Persuasion that lasts. Restraint that reads as confidence.
Oversized Playfair against small, wide-tracked labels — extreme size contrast — with a single phrase pivoted into italic gold. That italic-gold turn recurs across every headline.

Video

The studio’s on-screen identity: an antiqued title/end card that matches the coin exactly — flat cream masthead and gold coin on navy, with the same paper grain, no shimmer — plus lower-third nameplates. For lower-thirds over footage, drop the navy field and place the coin or nameplate as a corner bug so the gold and cream sit on the image directly.

06

Voice

Confident restraint. We are precise, plain-spoken, and allergic to hype. Active voice, sentence case, no filler. The typography says “premium”; the words don’t have to. When in doubt, say less.
